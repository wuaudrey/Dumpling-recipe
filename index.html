Homemade Pork Dumplings

Dumplings are a traditional dish eaten for Lunar New Year, but they're so good you'll want to eat them all year round! You can make them in large batches, freeze them and have them whenever you want!

  • Prep: 30 minutes
  • Cook: 15 minutes
  • Servings: 5 servings

Ingredients

Filling
Wrapping
Sauce
Pan Frying

Instructions

Making Pork Filling:

  1. In a large bowl, add pork, ginger, sesame oil, and salt. Stir until they combine into a smooth silky mixture.
  2. Add scallions and stir them into mixture.

Making Dumpling Wrapping:

  1. In a bowl, mix together flour and salt. Then add water and mix with wooden spoon/spatula.
  2. With your hands, start forming the dough. It will be hard at the beginning but keep kneading dough for about 5-7 minutes. Form the dough into a ball.
  3. When ready, place dough into plastic bag or cover in plastic wrap and let it rest for 15 minutes on the kitchen counter.
  4. Divide the dough into 2 equal pieces. Put one piece back in the bag. Roll the other half into a sausage shape and cut it into even pieces.
  5. Flatten each piece with a hand and roll them out as thin as possible.
  6. Scoop about 1 tabelspoon of pork filling onto a dumpling skin. Dip finger in water and run it along the round edges of the dumpling skin to moisten. Fold in half and pinch along the edges to seal.
  7. Fill, fold, repeat!

Frying the Dumplings:

  1. Heat canola oil in a nonstick pan over medium heat for a few minutes until hot.
  2. Working in batches, add dumplings to the pan in a single layer. Let them cook until browned on bottom for about 5 minutes.
  3. Pour about 2 tablespoons of water over dumplings and cover with a lid. Let them steam until cooked through (about 5 minutes).
  4. Remove the lid. Transfer dumplings to a serving plate.
  5. Combine dipping sauce ingredients in a dipping bowl. Serve with dumplings.
  6. Enjoy!
